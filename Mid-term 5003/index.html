Understanding Universal Facial Expressions

Making a first impression is crucial for all candidates as impressions tend to stick. One of the way impressions are formed is by the way a person evaluates the language of candidates. We look for candidates that exhibit nonverbal cues that reinforce our beliefs about how we feel about the candidates. This is called "Confirmation bias". Confirmation bias highlights the minds ability to focus on those aspects of a person that reinforce our preexisting beliefs. If we believe the candidate is "likeable" or "caring" we focus on those aspects of body language which reinforces our belief. For example, if we see body language such as smile or raised eyebrows we believe the candidate is empathetic or caring. There are seven universal facial expressions exhibited by all humans. Understanding the characteristics of each of these expressions while assist you in accurately interpreting what emotion the candidate is truly conveying. Understanding the universal expression can assist you in overcoming any potential "confirmation bias".

Universal Facial Expressions

Happy

A face expressing happiness has a smile with some teeth exposed, and a wrinkle runs from the outer nose to the outer corners of the lip. The cheeks are raised, and the lower eyelids are tense or wrinkled. The narrowing of the eyelids causes “crow’s feet” wrinkles at the outer corners of the eyes.

Sad

A face expressing sadness has the eyebrows drawn in and up, the skin below the eyebrows is triangulated with the inner corner up, and the corners of the lips are turned down. The jaw comes up and the bottom lip pouts out.

Fear

A face expressing fear has raised eyebrows that are usually flat. There are wrinkles in the forehead in the center between the brows. The upper eyelids are raised, but lower eyelids are tense and drawn up. The lips are usually tensed or drawn back, the mouth may be open and nostrils may be flared.

Suprise

A surprised face features the eyebrows raised up and curved. The skin below the brow is stretched and there are horizontal wrinkles across the forehead. The jaw is dropped and teeth are slightly parted, but there’s no stretching or tension of the mouth.

Disgust

A disgusted face has the eyebrows downcast, but the lower eyelid raised (causing the eyes to narrow), the cheeks are raised and the nose is scrunched. The upper lip is also raised or curled upward

Anger

An angry face will show eyebrows that are lowered and drawn together, eyes staring hard or bulging, with vertical lines appearing between the brows and the lower eyelids tensed. Nostrils may be flared, and the mouth is either firmly pressed together with the lips drawn down at the corners, or in a square shape as if shouting. The lower jaw juts out.

Contempt

A face showing contempt, has one corner of the mouth rising, like a sort of half-smile which is actually a sneer.
